What's New


From April 11th to 12th, I participated a Sustainable Case Competition organized by Copenhagen Business School. The case was proposed by Chr. Hansen to provide recommendations to make strides on SDG 12: Responsible consumption and production. Our team provided a solution “Triad partnership to reduce and optimize dairy package waste”.

From Feb. 2018, I have been working on an individual project-"Valuation of the System Advantages of Lower District Heating Temperatures" proposed by Sweco. I am supervised by Prof. Brian Elmegaard to examine how to create incentives for consumers to select Ultra-low temperature district heat system to achieve more sustainability.

On 27 Feb. 2018, the paper "An in-depth analysis of the silicon solar cell key parameters’ optimal magnitudes using PC1D simulations" was accepted by Optik-International Journal for Light and Electron Optics. In this study, the optimal magnitudes of silicon solar cell key parameters were calculated and verified using the PC1D simulation program, which proves that PC1D can provide reliable reference values for solar cell design.

In Dec. 2017, I finished a group project-"Life Cycle Assessment of Decentralised Toilets in comparison with Flushing and Composting Toilets". The project was supervised by NP Flint and DTU QSA to quantify the environmental impact of different toilets with SimaPro thus and identify the hotspot stage to improve current design.
