What's New


On Aug. 27th, I became a BD/Analysis Intern at ENGIE a global energy and services group, to cultivate my business savvy. I actively tracked market intelligence and changes in the policy, regulatory and competitive environment relevant to solid biomass sector, and create quantitative and qualitative reports analyzing the market potentials and risks. In the next three months, I will support the evaluation and delivery of commercial investment opportunities including origination, due diligence, deal structuring, negotiation, through to deal completion.

From Mar. to Jun. 2018, I accomplished an individual project-"Valuation of the System Advantages of Lower District Heating Temperatures" proposed by Sweco and supervised by Prof. Brian Elmegaard. The aim was to quantify the advantages and disadvantages of lower DH temperatures and perform a feasibility evaluation of the trainsition into (Ultra) low temperature district heating, covering financial, environmental, energy and socio-economic aspects. An Excel-based model had been developed to conduct calculations and strategy analysis. Report

On 27 Feb. 2018, the paper "An in-depth analysis of the silicon solar cell key parameters’ optimal magnitudes using PC1D simulations" was accepted by Optik-International Journal for Light and Electron Optics, supervised by Prof. Qingchun Yu. In this study, the optimal magnitudes of silicon solar cell key parameters were simulated using the PC1D program and the carrier transmission mechanism behind that was analyzed, which proves that PC1D can provide reliable reference values for solar cell design.

From April 11th to 12th, I participated a Sustainable Case Competition organized by Copenhagen Business School. The case was proposed by Chr. Hansen to provide recommendations to make strides on SDG 12: Responsible consumption and production. Our team provided a solution “Triad partnership to reduce and optimize dairy package waste”.
