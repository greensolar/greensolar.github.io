Research & Project work

Valuation of the System Advantages of Lower District Heating Temperatures  Feb. 2018-present

The project is proposed by Sweco and supervised by Prof. Brian Elmegaard . The aim is to quantify the advantages and disadvantages of lower district heating temperatures and perform a feasibility evaluation of the trainsition into (Ultra) low temperature district heat, covering financial, environmental, energy and socio-economic aspects. Report

Research on key design parameters of the silicon solar cell using PC1D Feb.2017-Jan. 2018

In this study supervised by Prof. Qingchun Yu, the optimal magnitudes of silicon solar cell key parameters were calculated and verified using the PC1D simulation program, which proves that PC1D can provide reliable reference values for solar cell design. The paper "An in-depth analysis of the silicon solar cell key parameters’ optimal magnitudes using PC1D simulations" was accepted by Optik-International Journal for Light and Electron Optics

Life Cycle Assessment of Decentralised Toilets Sep.-Dec. 2017

The project was supervised by NP Flint and DTU QSA to quantify the environmental impact of three different toilets, e.g. Decentralised, Flushing, composting Toilets with SimaPro and identify the hotspot stage to improve current design. Report

Investigate on EU and China Carbon Market Jun.-Aug. 2017

The project was supervised by Prof. Xiaomin Xie to investigate the determinations of carbon price, especially the renewable energy factor based on EU ETS and provide policy advice to China ETS. Presentation

Solar Decathlon China 2018  Mar. 2016-April. 2017

As a former Energy System Department Member of Team SJTU-UIUC, I designed and simulated the PV system for a double-decker, solar-powered permanent family house with PVsyst. Report

The study of collaborative design with multi-disciplinary BIM softwares Feb. 2016-Mar. 2017

The study was supervised by Prof. Xueyuan Deng. As the group leader, I took charge of HVAC Modeling of SJTU library and explored the information exchange among MagiCAD, ArchiCAD, Navisworks, Bimsight, etc. And based on this project, the BIM team in SJTU won the first prize in 2016 National Building Information Modeling Contest. Defence presentation
